Bergamasco

The Bergamasco was found in Italy as a shepherds right hand man to protect the flock. The Bergamasco independency made is a great problem solver when tending to the herd of sheep. The uniqueness quality of the Bergamasco is its coat. Containing dog hair, goat hair, and wool, it creates a black and gray color for the mats that can be long enough to touch the ground over 6 years of age. The Bergamasco also have extremely long eyes lashes to protect their eyes from their fur on top of their heads, while the fur protects it from snow blindness. The Bergamasco is also capable of high acute hearing and psychic awareness around its environment. They would be very reliable on to detect things approaching or violent behavior.

The Bergamasco is a very sociable animal. It was capable of having independent relationship with each person it lives with. The Bergamasco also< underneath all that coat, is an athletic dog which weighs around 50-75 lbs. Its origins go back to almost nearly 7,000 years to a place what is now called Iran. It is officially called The Shepards Dog

  • The Bergamasco Sheepdog is an ancient breed that is at least 2000 years old.
  • The Bergamascos matted coat is meant to protect him from bad weather and the predators
  • The Bergamasco Sheepdog is best suited for seasonal to cold climates
